1. Layers and Shapes

You have three layers at your disposal: Layer 1, Layer 2, and Layer 3. Think of each layer as a musical track where you can create your melodies and rhythms. Click and drag within a layer to draw shapes. These shapes represent sound! The width of the shape controls the duration, and the vertical position controls the pitch.

2. Control Panel

  • Play, Pause, Stop: Start, pause, or stop your soundscape at any time.
  • Loop: Turn looping on or off to repeat your sounds continuously.
  • Tempo: Adjust the speed of the playhead and the rhythm of your composition.
  • Clear: Enter clear mode to delete shapes, or use ‚ÄúClear All‚Äù to start fresh.

3. Sound Effects

Each layer has toggle buttons for Amplitude, Panning, Reverb, and Distortion. Switch these on to reveal sliders that allow you to customize your sound further.
The toggles apply to the entire layer, so all shapes in that layer will be affected by the settings.

4. Drawing and Editing

  • Draw Mode: Turn drawing mode on or off. When on, click and drag to create shapes. When off, you can move or edit existing shapes.
  • Dragging Shapes: Once a shape is drawn, feel free to drag it around to adjust its position and sound properties.
